Let's do some math!

A Galapagos Tortoise can walk at a top speed of 0.3 km/hour (Galapagos tortoise).
A Galapagos Tortoise can live for over 100 years (GalÃ¡pagos tortoise)
The earth has a circumference of 40,075km (Earth).

Let's say it will only walk at 1/2 that speed on average (account for resting, eating, not running the whole way, etc.). How many hours a day would it have to walk to make it around the earth?

40,075 km / 0.15  (km/hour) = 313,834 hours
100 years * 365 days/year = 36,500 days
313,834 hours / 36,500 days = 8.6 hours/day

The Galapagos Tortoise can nap up to 16 hours/day, but that's still not so much that it couldn't keep up with it's 8.6 hours/day.
So a tortoise that could:
  • Live a bit more then 100 years
  • Sleep a little less during the day
  • Keep up it's pace

Would probably be able to travel around the world!

This means that it would take it 6,184 days, or roughly 17 years. But that is without sleeping or any rest. An knowing that tortoises can live up to 180-190 years, I can say YES!
